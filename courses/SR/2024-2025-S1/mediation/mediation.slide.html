Mediation

In statistics, a mediation model is one that seeks to identify and explain the mechanism or process that underlies an observed relationship between an independent variable and a dependent variable via the inclusion of a third hypothetical variable, known as a mediator variable (also a mediating variable, intermediary variable, or intervening variable) (Wikipedia, 2024).

Causality

Mediation does not imply causation!

Criteria for causal relations

All requirements need to be met:

  1. Correlation
  2. Time order
  3. No spurious (false) correlation
  4. No common cause confounder

Example

Does the speed of recovery after sickness improve with the use of alternative medicine or is this effect mediated by a healthy lifestyle?
